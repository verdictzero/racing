A nested RACI is one work breakdown with a RACI grid at every level, threaded by a single rule: the Responsible for a parent becomes the Accountable for its children.

The problemWhy not just one big grid?

A flat RACI is perfect for one team on one project: rows are tasks, columns are people, cells are R / A / C / I. It falls apart the moment the work is a hierarchy.

Flatten a whole Portfolio into a single sheet and you get thousands of rows, every stakeholder as a column, and no sign of the org structure. Nobody can read it — and worse, you can't see who actually owns what. So instead of one sheet, you keep a small matrix at each tier, and each row opens into the matrix one level down.

ReferenceThe four letters

Every row gets exactly one A. The rest can repeat. Keep this key handy — the whole model rides on the difference between R and A.

A

Accountable the ownerone per row

Owns the outcome and answers for it. Exactly one per row — this is the spine of the whole cascade.

R

Responsible the doercascades ↓

Does the actual work. At the next level down, this person becomes the A — that's what makes it nest.

C

Consulted two-way

Gives input before the work happens; a real back-and-forth, not just a heads-up.

I

Informed one-way

Kept in the loop after the fact. No action required, no reply expected.

The moveWhat makes it nest, not just group

Grouping rows into folders isn't nesting. Nesting is a hand-off with a rule:

“Doing” a Program really means owning the delivery of its Projects. So accountability flows downhill — your job as the doer of the parent becomes your ownership of its children. Do that at every boundary and you get one unbroken thread of accountability: every level has exactly one owner, and any task traces straight up to the person who owns the whole portfolio.

PayoffWhy bother nesting?

Five things you get for the price of the extra structure.

Readable

Each grid stays small. You drill to detail instead of scrolling a wall of rows.

Delegation is visible

You can point at exactly where accountability is handed off — the R→A boundary between two levels.

Nothing gets orphaned

Every task rolls up to exactly one owner, all the way to the top. No work without an owner.

Mirrors the org

Columns are real org units, and drilling matches how the work — and the org — actually decompose together.

Change stays local

Re-plan one Project and you touch one small matrix. The Portfolio sheet never moves.

It rolls up

Because the thread is unbroken, health and status at the bottom can total upward into an honest portfolio view.

Gut-checkBefore you model one

Two rules keep a nested RACI honest

One A per row. If a row has zero owners, nobody's accountable; if it has two, nobody is. Exactly one, every time.

The child's owner should be the parent's doer. When a row you drilled into gets an A that isn't the parent's R, that's a deliberate branch override — allowed, but worth a second look. It means accountability jumped tracks, so make sure that was on purpose.
